Chapter Two

When he got to the office, Jake felt somewhat calmer but still a little spooked. He went to the kitchen area, poured a mug of coffee and then hunkered down in his cubicle, trying to keep out of sight. He scanned quickly through his cryptomails. These didn't contain anything out of the ordinary so, as was his usual habit, he scanned quickly through the Reuters, Wall Street Journal and BBC RSS feeds on his display wall. These didn't reveal anything out of the ordinary. North American troops continued to suffer heavy casualties in the ground offensive east of Vienna and had only been saved from complete annihilation by the EU forces when the Russians halted the advancing European forces with drone strikes. Nearer to home, two peace protesters camped on the Mall in DC had been shot by a Québécois terrorist who was later apprehended and shot dead by the FBI outside a Winnipeg fast food restaurant. Jake shuddered and wondered at how rapidly things had changed from the relatively settled era before the war.
