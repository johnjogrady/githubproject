Chapter One

Jake awoke with a start to realise that the alarm on his phone had been sounding for at least 5 minutes and was now kicking up a hell of a racket. Xavi, his half wild, ancient cat lay at the end of the bed. The cat opened one eye, gave Jake a disinterested glare, and yawned expressively, showing yellowing teeth.

Jake's t-shirt felt damp, and smelled badly of tumbler of bourbon he remembered pouring just before he fell asleep

He fell onto the tiled floor beside his bed and realised that hed located the tumbler, which was now in pieces on the tiled floor. He stood up and felt a wincing pain in his foot from the broken glass

He hobbled to the kitchen, attended to his lacerated foot and made some coffee.

The apartment felt unbelievably cold, and looking out to the street, he realised that last night's wind and sleet had turned to snow and everywhere was covered in a blanket of pristine whiteness

Jake shivered and made his way to the bathroom and commenced his daily ablutions...

Twenty minutes later, while walking down the steps into the subway, his phone buzzed in his pocket. It was Donnelly. He silenced the call and felt a wave of panic wash over him. He wondered at this unusual impulse to avoid his old friend and in particular, how he would explain his strange behaviour He would only lose signal somewhere under Rockaway like he always did. His ancient iPhone, a cracked and battered relic from back in the days of the Obama presidency, barely worked these days

He closed his eyes and tried to breathe slowly to calm himself
